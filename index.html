Curve-ascending

`This is a curve-ascending bomsai, this form of bomsai has a trunk that runs upwards in an S shape. this style is always made by guiding young plant material past one or two sticks that are placed in the open ground. This way it grows into a S from.

Broom-style

The broom style bomsai. Here the young plant material is allowed to grow in length until the trunk is thick enough, after it is cut the crown can develop through new roots from the trunk, this style is faster at the end result and is also less labor-intensive

Hangin-style

The hangin-style Bonsai starts off as a curved ascending style. Only from repotting it is possible to let the crown grow lower than the root. The tree is also potted at an angle after which the branches can be cultivated. this style needs less time to get the full crown and is also relatively more expensive than the other styles
